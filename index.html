Complaints Policy

How to raise a concern about anyone acting on behalf of Blues Engineering — officers, executives, or members representing the club.

Last updated: July 14, 2026

Read the full policy → Email the Ombudsperson →
01

Who you can complain about

Officers, Project Directors (and members of their team), Board of Directors members, and Affiliated Club members — including anyone on the Blues Engineering exec acting in an official capacity.

02

Valid reasons for a complaint

  • Harassment, sexual violence, defamation, or slander
  • Failure to carry out their role
  • Violations of the U of T Code of Student Conduct or the EngSoc Standards of Affiliation
03

Can it be anonymous?

Yes — unless your identity as the complainant is necessary to investigate the complaint.

04

What your complaint needs

Enough detail for the Ombudsperson to begin an investigation.

05

Possible responses

  • No action taken (insufficient proof, not misconduct, or outside the role's time period)
  • An offer to mediate a solution
  • A factual recommendation report, decided by vote of the relevant executive body (typically the Club Affiliation Committee or Board of Directors), with privacy protected throughout

The investigator cannot take executive action on their own — only recommend.

06

Possible recommendations

  • Recall the subject from their position
  • Action under Section 0.9 of the Policy on Affiliated Clubs
  • Mandated additional training
  • A required official apology
  • Escalation to campus police, law enforcement, the Faculty Dean, Student Life, or the University Provost
  • Amendments to EngSoc Bylaws or Policies
  • Any combination of the above, or another justified action
